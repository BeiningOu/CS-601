I originally come from China. I had my bachelor's degree at the South China University of Technology in 2016, majoring in Food Science and Engineering. After college, I went to Penn State to pursue my first M.S. degree in Agriculture and Biological Engineering (2017 Fall -2019 Spring).

At Penn State, I worked in Dr. Ali Demirci's lab and did my microbiology research. That is where I learned to become a researcher---writing proposals, designing and conducting experiments, data collection and analysis, technical writing, and presentations. Most importantly, how to think like a researcher. After two year's research and study, I graduated in 2019 Spring and published three papers and one book chapter with my advisor Dr. Ali Demirci.

After Penn State, I worked at Xenon Corporation for one year and a half. At Xenon, I was with the engineering team, doing research and customized testing. My responsibility includes developing test protocols for different biological challenges; performing biological testing; writing reports and presenting internally and externally.

With great interest and passion for software and programming, I decided to get another M.S degree in software development. Therefore, I got into Boston University and now I am in my last year of the program. Courses I took at Boston University include “information structures with python”, “data structures and algorithms”, “software design and patterns”, “information systems analysis and design” and “web application development” …

Now I live in a Boston suburb -- Methuen, MA -- with my husband, our boy, and our cat. It is a small town and very convenient in terms of living and transportation. I love cooking Chinese food, especially spicy food! When we got some free time, I will play piano and sing songs. My favorite family activity recently is going to a playground called "Filed of Dream" in NH.
